FULL SCENE TEXT (ARCHIVED)
=========================================

The lighting in The Warehouse was warm, low — the kind that turned wood soft and made strangers look like lovers. Carl liked it. There was a corner table that let him keep his back to the wall, Riley within reach, and Jenna drifting in and out of his line of sight like some sort of calm tide.

Riley had her boots up on the seat next to her, lazy and glowing, one hand wrapped around a sweating glass of whatever Jenna had slid her from the bar. She was mid-laugh, something soft and wicked, when the front door cracked open.

And there she was.

Cassie.

All hips and heat and danger in a stolen leather jacket and jeans that were borderline illegal. The door didn’t open so much as *yield* to her. Her copper curls caught the light like they’d been waiting for it. She didn’t walk in. She *detonated*.

Carl coughed once. Low. Audible. Like a warning shot.
Riley blinked. “…well shit.”

Cassie spotted them immediately.

Her smile was slow and carnivorous.

“Oh, *look* what I stepped into,” she purred, weaving between tables like the bar owed her something. “Y’all having a date night? That’s *precious*.”

Jenna appeared behind the bar at that exact moment, towel in one hand, posture just a touch too still. Her eyes flicked up once. Tracked Cassie like a weather system.

Carl leaned back in his chair, expression unreadable. “Evenin’, Cassie.”
Cassie’s eyes sparkled. “*Carl.*”
Then turned. “*Riley.*”

Riley raised her glass in a lazy salute, legs crossed, voice sugar-slick. “Didn’t think you liked this place. Not enough trouble to cause.”

Cassie laughed — full and shameless. “You wound me, baby. Maybe I came for the food.”
She leaned closer to the table, palms flat. “Or maybe I just have excellent timing.”

Carl didn’t blink, but Riley saw it — the faint twitch in his jaw. The way his hand on the table curled into a fist before relaxing again.

Behind them, Jenna was still as a held breath.

Cassie leaned in just far enough to whisper, but aimed it square at Riley.

“I didn’t know y’all were... nesting.”

Riley grinned — bright, sharp, no mercy. “You’re late, Red. I already set the whole house on fire.”
Cassie smiled like a secret. “Didn’t say I came to put it out.”

Jenna moved.

Not fast. Not loud. But with purpose.

She crossed behind Carl’s chair, handed Riley a fresh drink without asking, then set a plate of fries between them with a soft, “Figured you could use something solid.”

“Thanks, sweetheart,” Riley said, and her hand brushed Jenna’s briefly — on purpose.

Cassie watched it.

Watched *her.*

For a moment, her eyes narrowed — not angry. Not even jealous. Just... curious. Unsettled. Like she couldn’t figure out if Jenna was a threat or just a deeper level of the game.

Jenna finally looked her way. Calm. Polite.
“You looking to order, ma’am?”

Cassie blinked. Blinked again.

“Oh,” she said slowly. “*You’re* the one.”
She smiled. “Well, well. Now it’s interesting.”

Carl shifted in his chair. Just once. Enough for Riley to feel it — the tension, the warning. She tapped her boot against his ankle.

*Don’t. Not yet.*

Cassie grinned wide.

“You always serve him fries with a side of your thighs,” she asked Jenna, voice sweet as sugarcane, “or just when you’re off-duty?”

Jenna tilted her head slightly. Smiled like a blade.
“No one’s had to ask for seconds yet.”

Carl choked.
Riley full-on *howled* with laughter.

Cassie blinked, caught off guard, then sat back with a slow grin.

“Well hell,” she said, raising a hand. “I’ll take a whiskey. Neat. And a seat where I can keep watch in case y’all combust.”

Jenna nodded. “Bar’s wide open.”
Cassie winked. “So am I.”
