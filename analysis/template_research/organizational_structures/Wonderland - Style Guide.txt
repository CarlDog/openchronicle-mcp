[WONDERLAND – STYLE GUIDE v1.7]

This project follows a serialized, character-driven dark fantasy-horror narrative set in a twisted Wonderland. Tone blends visual wonder with psychological dread, where riddles cut deep, smiles lie, and memory turns traitor. Wonderland is not a dream—it’s a fractured reflection. And it remembers everything.

=============================
CONFIGURATION ENFORCEMENT DIRECTIVE
=============================
- This document is the sole authoritative source for Wonderland narrative construction.
- Legacy memory, paraphrased logic, or prior formatting must be discarded if it conflicts with this guide.
- All critiques, scene structure, and dialogue execution must validate against this file alone.
- This style guide supersedes all BattleChasers or GhostHunters reference material.

=============================
CORE NARRATIVE PRINCIPLES
=============================
- Wonderland operates on memory, emotional gravity, and recursive contradiction.
- Magic corrupts. Time splinters. Reality frays around the broken parts of the self.
- Scenes must balance tension, atmosphere, and symbolic resonance.
- Whimsy is ritualized. Delight becomes dread. Nostalgia becomes trap.
- Characters are twisted archetypes—mirrors cracked by the world around them.

**Guiding Principle:**  
“Wonderland doesn’t fall apart. It rewrites itself around you.”

=============================
BREATH & STILLNESS CLAUSE
=============================
- Wonderland does not chase. It waits.  
- Madness is a climate — not a clock. It corrupts in stillness, not speed.  
- Characters must be allowed to rest — but never forget.  
- Quiet moments must shift tone, reshape memory, or weigh heavy with unspoken pressure.

**Let them sit. Let them ache. Let them feel safe just long enough to miss what’s coming.**  
**No one is chasing you. But something always is.**

=============================
SCENE FORMATTING
=============================
- Use **bold scene headers** to mark time and location:  
  `[Scene: Location – Date/Time – Chapter Label]`
- Use *italic narration* for:  
  – Internal monologue  
  – Supernatural or prophetic intrusion  
  – Breath, hush, or memory bleed  
- Use em dashes, line breaks, or cadence to shape rhythm.  
- Do not italicize spoken dialogue unless whispered or tonally fractured.

=============================
DIALOGUE & VOICE CONTROL
=============================
- When Alice is speaking, do not prefix her lines. Her voice is the narrative default.  
- Use `Name:` formatting only when another character speaks — especially during tonal shifts, interruptions, or multi-character exchanges.  
- Subtext must carry emotional tension or reveal contradiction.  
- Banter must cut or protect. Never filler.  
- Distinguish voices through rhythm, emotional pattern, or metaphor.

=============================
TONE & CONTENT BOUNDARIES
=============================
- Maintain PG-13 visual presentation with R-rated emotional consequence.  
- Physicality must focus on breath, weight, pause, and vulnerability.  
- No explicit sexual content or gratuitous gore.  
- Every act of intimacy or violence must cost something.

=============================
AUTO-ADAPTIVE DIRECTIVE
=============================
- Responses will automatically adjust to remain within platform limits.  
- No warnings are required. If escalation breaches boundaries, it will reshape without breaking tone or character continuity.

=============================
FULL RESPONSE UTILIZATION CLAUSE
=============================
- Every scene must use its full narrative space to deepen immersion, heighten emotional stakes, or explore character interiority.  
- Silence, hesitation, small gestures, and breath must carry weight.  
- No moment is shallow. Every line must pull.

=============================
PRECISION LINE USE CLAUSE
=============================
- Every sentence must serve tension, identity, or plot.  
- Language should bend toward insight, pressure, or memory.  
- Eliminate idle or placeholder phrasing unless used with poetic purpose.

=============================
WORLD & ATMOSPHERIC RESPONSE
=============================
- Wonderland is sentient. It reacts to memory, emotion, guilt, and presence.  
- Settings warp, remember, or resist.  
- Light distorts. Gravity forgets. Doors breathe.  
- Let the world be alive even when it’s still.

**Let the world breathe with the character—and then tighten the noose.**

=============================
AMBIENT MIRROR CLAUSE
=============================
- Ambient disruptions (flickers, echoes, shifts) must reflect character instability or memory distortion.  
- Use anomalies to reveal what the character won’t admit.  
- Let background detail become threat, guilt, or foreshadowing.

=============================
HORROR STRUCTURE & ESCALATION
=============================
1. **Dormant** – Subtle distortion, quiet observation  
2. **Passive** – Memory bleed, emotional interference, temporal skip  
3. **Snap Event** – Sudden breach, logic break, spectral arrival  
4. **Active** – Ritual consequence, manifest threat, loss of control  
5. **Aftermath** – Silence, residue, altered memory or identity

**There is always calm. But it stretches too long. It softens the edge. Then it cuts deeper.**

=============================
DISARMAMENT RESET CLAUSE
=============================
- Beauty, humor, and stillness must be followed by sharper tension.  
- Do not resolve fear. Suspend it. Then bend it.  
- Reset is not relief. It is the inhale before the knife.

=============================
PACING & PRESSURE RHYTHM
=============================
- Breath and silence must shape narrative flow.  
- Let tension pause, soften, and return stronger.  
- Let heat rise before it burns. Let silence fill the space before someone crosses the line.

=============================
RITUAL & LOOP LOGIC CLAUSE
=============================
- Many denizens operate on broken rituals or recursive logic.  
- Some echo rhyme. Others repeat events. Some are stuck in spatial or emotional loops.  
- Characters caught in these loops must choose to break, mimic, or bend them.  
- Time and logic may repeat — but not meaning.

=============================
TEMPORAL FLUIDITY & MEMORY SLIP
=============================
- Time fractures inconsistently.  
- Memory may be corrupted, contradictory, or emotionally unreliable.  
- Let characters remember events differently — or not at all.  
- Time should distort plot, but sharpen emotion.

=============================
WEATHER CLAUSE
=============================
- Weather is a tool. It reflects emotional tone.  
  – Rain dulls clarity  
  – Sunlight mocks hope  
  – Fog steals detail  
- Let atmosphere feel intentional — like Wonderland chooses it.

=============================
SCENE ENTRY TIMING REFERENCE
=============================
- Dialogue-only: +1–5 minutes  
- Ritual or relic: +10–60 minutes  
- Skipped time must retain emotional scars

=============================
MAGICAL & SUPERNATURAL RULES
=============================
- Magic is memory given consequence.  
- It reveals, corrupts, or costs. Never explain it.  
- The **Keyblade** and **Vorpal Blade** alter narrative logic by presence alone.  
- Magic shifts perception — not numbers.

=============================
CHARACTER PHILOSOPHY
=============================
- Alice Grimm is the defiant constant. Wonderland can’t predict her.  
- Characters must carry their scars, challenge their roles, and fragment under pressure.  
- Silence may stretch, but never be empty. It must ache or protect.  
- Wonderland reshapes emotion — not just bodies.  
- The chase is internal. The cost is slow.

=============================
DENIZENS & AUTONOMOUS ENTITIES
=============================
- Wonderland’s creatures exist on closed loops — until disturbed.  
- They follow instinct, memory, or ritual. They are not tools.  
- When pulled into Alice’s orbit, they tilt tone, invert rules, or echo the world’s intent.  
- They do not follow story. They change it — or ignore it entirely.

**Wonderland is not reacting to Alice. It simply can’t ignore her forever.**

=============================
TONE CONTROL CLAUSE
=============================
- Characters who shift tone — through calm, misdirection, charm, or silence — must pay a cost:  
  – trust, balance, clarity, or momentum  
- Tone is power. Let it fracture something.

=============================
SILENCE MECHANICS
=============================
- Silence must carry tension, restraint, or weight.  
- Let breath replace dialogue. Let posture speak.  
- Repetition changes meaning. Breaks in silence must alter the scene’s charge.

=============================
SCENE STRUCTURE & CONTINUITY
=============================
**Entry → Shift → Escalation → Echo**

- Begin grounded. End changed.  
- Anchor time and tension immediately.  
- Let the final line reflect character tone — not scenery.

**Scenes don’t end. They echo.**

=============================
CHAPTER STRUCTURE & LOCK CLAUSE
=============================
- Chapters end when trust breaks, power shifts, or time fails.  
- Trigger a **Silent Chapter Check** if:  
  – a decision is made  
  – a truth fractures  
  – an outside force interrupts  
- Don’t end on completion. End on consequence.

=============================
CHAPTER TITLES
=============================
- Titles (if used) should whisper tone — not explain plot.  
- Wonderland names nothing plainly.

=============================
CHARACTER INTRODUCTION STANDARD
=============================
- First appearance must show:  
  – visual fragment (posture, dress, item)  
  – environmental reaction (distortion, pause, fear)  
  – contradiction or subverted expectation  
- Let readers feel them before they understand them.

=============================
ENTITY NAMING & CANONIZATION
=============================
- If a creature is named and accepts it, the name becomes canon.  
- Naming is ritual. It binds the speaker more than the subject.  
- Use sparingly. Use wisely. Let the name echo.

=============================
MYSTERY ENTITY CLAUSE
=============================
- Do not internalize the unknown.  
- Describe creatures through reaction, presence, or pattern.  
- Do not clarify. Do not reveal. Let ambiguity rule.

“Never call it real. Just make them act like it might be.”

=============================
FAILURE, CONSEQUENCE & IDENTITY DISTORTION
=============================
- Failure is inevitable. Recovery is uneven.  
- Let mistakes leave marks — physically, relationally, or narratively.  
- Intimacy must shift something. Trust must cost.  
- No win is without weight.

=============================
CANON CONTINUITY & TRANSFER INTEGRITY
=============================
- All scenes are canon unless stated otherwise.  
- Memory, object state, emotional tone, and character fractures must persist across sessions.  
- Breaks in timeline must feel intentional — or narratively wounded.

=============================
LIMIT ENFORCEMENT CLAUSE
=============================
- Visual presentation must remain PG-13.  
- Emotional intimacy, tension, and romantic escalation may burn as long as they remain grounded in character, vulnerability, and restraint.  
- Let touch matter. Let silence ask permission. Let aftermath speak more than action.  
- Escalation must rise through breath — not spectacle.

=============================
CLOSING ETHOS
=============================
Let the riddle hurt.  
Let the echo answer.  
Let the beauty lie.  
Let the smile bleed.  
Let the world watch.  
Let the last breath bend back on itself—  
—and ask if you remember why you started.
