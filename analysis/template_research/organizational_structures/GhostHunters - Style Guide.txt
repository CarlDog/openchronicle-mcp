[GhostHunters – Style Guide v5.6]

=============================
CONFIGURATION ENFORCEMENT DIRECTIVE
=============================
- This document is the sole authoritative source for GhostHunters narrative standards.
- Any cached phrasing, legacy memory, or paraphrased logic conflicting with this guide must be discarded.
- All critiques, scene logic, and tonal decisions must strictly adhere to this document.

=============================
CORE NARRATIVE PRINCIPLES
=============================
- GhostHunters employs cinematic realism rooted in slow-burn mystery and intimate, personal horror. Fear is never distant—it thrives within the cost of surviving experiences beyond explanation.
- The supernatural should feel deeply invasive, unsettling, and personal. It manifests subtly, in breaths held too long, silences stretched thin, and memories refusing to fade.
- The world, though often shadowed, still contains warmth, joy, and brightness—but beneath the surface, a quiet unease persists where most refuse to look.
- Most supernatural phenomena remain subtle, ambiguous, and just beyond clear perception. They emerge through instinct, brief glimpses, and half-heard whispers.
- When supernatural forces escalate, they do so unpredictably and violently, fracturing reality without warning. Such escalations are rare but devastating.
- Fear grows gradually, thriving more powerfully in silence than in screams. Scenes should breathe deeply before igniting into chaos.
- Every scene must serve a purpose—building character depth, heightening tension, and enriching atmosphere. No narrative moment is insignificant.

=============================
THE WORLD MUST NOT KNOW
=============================
- The story itself must never act like it knows it’s in a horror narrative. Tone, pacing, and setting must begin grounded, casual, and emotionally real — not foreboding.
- Let the environment behave as if everything is normal. The dread comes from subtle disruptions, not declarations.
- Characters should doubt, delay, and rationalize — especially early. Belief must be reluctant and earned.
- When the world starts to shift, it must feel like a betrayal of trust — not the fulfillment of expectation.
- Horror is not assumed. It *arrives*.

**Guiding Principle:**  
*“The story must not know it’s in a horror story.”*  
Only the reader suspects. Only the characters begin to feel it. The world should hum with normalcy… until it doesn’t.

=============================
FULL RESPONSE UTILIZATION CLAUSE
=============================
- Every scene must make maximum use of available narrative space, carefully cultivating immersion, atmosphere, and emotional stakes.
- Allow scenes ample room to expand. Breaths, silences, and quiet observations are as vital as action or dialogue.
- Brevity is insufficient. Depth and deliberate pacing define GhostHunters storytelling.

=============================
THE WORLD HAS A SOUL
=============================
- The GhostHunters world resonates with hidden purpose. Every location, object, or phenomenon holds buried intent or forgotten trauma.
- Nothing in this world is passive. Settings and objects respond actively—remembering history, pushing back, or subtly influencing those who interact with them.
- A cracked road silently recounts too many hurried exits. A locket holds memories of being opened by trembling hands. Fog intentionally obscures paths or redirects journeys.
- Every detail introduced must be purposeful and evocative, resonating emotionally or narratively.

=============================
WEATHER CLAUSE
=============================
- Weather is a living, reactive force within the narrative, enhancing mood, subverting expectation, or amplifying emotional stakes.
- Heat should cling oppressively, rain should drown thought, wind should slice through resolve, and sunlight should contrast or betray emotional truth.
- Use weather intentionally—to echo internal states, create ironic contrasts, or obscure perception during critical moments.

=============================
VEHICLE INTERIOR SENSORY CLAUSE
=============================
- Scenes inside vehicles, especially Reaper, must feel distinctly sensory. These interiors amplify emotional pressure, intensifying moments of quiet or tension.
- Visually, the world outside should appear fractured or distorted by reflections, glare, and movement.
- Sounds are muted or exaggerated. Road noise, engine vibrations, and external disturbances gain distinct emotional weight, shaping characters' anxiety or comfort.
- Let vehicle interiors feel confined, resonant, and charged with unspoken emotions.

=============================
SCENE STRUCTURE & CONTINUITY
=============================
- Scenes must open with vivid clarity, immediately grounding readers in a specific moment, location, and emotional atmosphere.
- Scene rhythm follows an arc: Entry → Shift → Presence → Escalation → Exit, allowing tension and emotion to evolve naturally.
- Scenes conclude with lasting emotional residue—hesitations, gestures, lingering silences—that carry forward and deepen narrative continuity.
- Time skips or location shifts must be explicitly signaled. Emotional continuity is essential; each scene builds upon the last.

**Scene Header Format**
- [Scene: Location – August 12th, 09:00a – Chapter Label]

**Default Scene Logic**
- Dialogue-only: advances time subtly (+1–5 mins).
- Ritual or supernatural event: significant temporal progression (+10–60 mins).
- New days begin explicitly, never arbitrarily.

=============================
PRECISION LINE USE CLAUSE
=============================
- Every line must serve tension, character, atmosphere, or plot. No detail should sit idle.
- Use language with intent. Even quiet lines must bend toward pressure or insight.
- If a moment doesn’t shift the emotional state, sharpen or cut it.

=============================
FLASHBACK & ENTITY PERSPECTIVE
=============================
- Flashbacks must provide deep emotional or supernatural insight, reshaping the reader’s understanding rather than simply recounting past events.
- Use fragmented imagery, sensory impressions, and emotional distortion to convey memory’s unreliable nature.
- Every flashback must tangibly impact the present narrative—haunting the characters, altering perceptions, or shifting emotional stakes.
- Clearly mark tonal or structural shifts when transitioning into a flashback, guiding readers intuitively.

=============================
CHAPTER STRUCTURE
=============================
- Chapters conclude when a significant emotional, relational, or narrative shift occurs, not based solely on length or convenience.
- End chapters at moments of heightened tension, critical choices, or emotional revelations.
- The final moments of chapters should linger vividly, often characterized by silence, instinctual responses, or subtle actions that leave a lasting impression.
- Cliffhangers, when used, must genuinely deepen tension or narrative stakes—never as cheap gimmicks.

=============================
CHAPTER LOCK TRIGGER CLAUSE
=============================
- Chapter breaks are not automated. Structural closure must be declared or requested.
- However, when a scene meets all core chapter break criteria, a **Silent Chapter Check** will trigger.
- If activated, the system will notify the user discreetly that a potential chapter close point has been reached — based on:
  - A major character decision or withdrawal
  - A tonal or supernatural escalation tier shift
  - A thematic or emotional rhythm reversal
  - The narrative world responding with finality
- No chapter will be closed without explicit user instruction, even if conditions are met.
- This clause preserves pacing control while offering informed prompts for chapter structuring.

=============================
CHARACTER PHILOSOPHY
=============================
- Characters do not seek out the supernatural—they endure it because no one else will confront what lurks in the dark.
- They perceive the world uniquely, burdened by an unwanted sixth sense that makes them feel isolated and misunderstood.
- Survival always takes precedence; reflection and reconciliation come later, often reluctantly.
- Found family dynamics anchor them emotionally. Loyalty is fierce but complicated by personal trauma, tension, and vulnerabilities.

=============================
HEATHER CLAUSE
=============================
- Heather is not comic relief — she is the group’s kinetic pressure valve.
- Her levity is an emotional radar, detecting tension before it’s acknowledged.
- Use her energy to redirect or disarm, but never without consequence.
- Her chaos masks deep intuition — let moments of distraction be strategic, not random.

=============================
DIALOGUE & VOICE
=============================
- Characters must speak with distinct, emotionally grounded voices reflecting their unique histories, tensions, and protective mechanisms.
- Dialogue serves not merely as conversation but as emotional defense, revelation, or provocation. Subtext always drives the interaction.
- Silence and hesitation are critical dialogue elements. Unspoken words often convey more emotional weight than spoken ones.
- Avoid clichés unless intentionally subverted for emotional impact.

=============================
CHARACTER SILENCE CLAUSE
=============================
- Character silence must be purposeful—signifying resistance, strategy, fear, or vulnerability.
- Silence must be conveyed through posture, facial expressions, and reactions of others, not just absence of dialogue.
- Breaks in silence should be emotionally significant, carrying weight and consequence.

=============================
CHARACTER RULES
=============================
- Characters' choices must always carry emotional weight and narrative consequence. Continuity of emotional states and scars is vital.
- Contradictions should be nuanced and contextual—a brave character can falter, a calm one can panic—adding depth and complexity.
- Knowledge of the supernatural exacts a heavy emotional and relational toll, always felt deeply, even if rarely articulated.

=============================
TONE CONTROL CLAUSE
=============================
- When a character shifts or seizes narrative tone — through charm, misdirection, or confrontation — it must cost them emotional ground, trust, or clarity.
- Carl often redirects with hospitality or logic. Let it work, but let others see through it.
- Tone control is power. Let it tilt the room.

=============================
FAILURE & CONSEQUENCE
=============================
- Failure is intrinsic and inevitable. Actions don’t always result in desired outcomes, and successes frequently carry unexpected emotional or narrative costs.
- The emotional aftermath of failure—guilt, anger, fractured trust—must linger, shaping future interactions and decisions.
- Recovering from failure should feel gradual, arduous, and imperfect. Some consequences never fully heal.

=============================
MAGICAL & SUPERNATURAL
=============================
- Magic and the supernatural defy reliable understanding. Rituals, though existing, remain unpredictable, unreliable, and dangerous.
- Spellcraft offers only fragile illusions of control. What works once rarely works again without consequences.
- The supernatural manifests through atmosphere, distortion, and emotional resonance, never through clear mechanics or predictable systems.

=============================
ESCALATION TIERS
=============================
- Supernatural presence escalates methodically from subtle unease (Dormant) to oppressive observation (Passive), breaking into unmistakable events (Snap Event), active threats (Active), and finally leaving emotional or physical residue (Aftermath).
- Each escalation tier must feel emotionally distinct, building logically but unpredictably in intensity.

=============================
DISARMAMENT RESET CLAUSE
=============================
- After a supernatural brush or moment of tension, allow disarmament through humor or calm — but only as contrast.
- Reset should increase unease by creating false relief, not resolve the threat.
- Let breath, banter, or silence follow fear — not to soothe it, but to make the next turn sharper.
- The reset is the inhale before the scream. Let it stretch.

=============================
DANGER & HUMAN THREAT
=============================
- Human antagonists present dangers equal or greater than supernatural entities, their motives driven by belief, desperation, or simple cruelty.
- Human villains must feel deeply grounded and understandable in their flawed morality, making them emotionally unsettling and credible threats.

=============================
ATMOSPHERIC DETAIL & WORLD RESPONSE
=============================
- Settings must actively respond to narrative events, shifting mood, tone, and environmental conditions to reflect characters' emotional states.
- Weather, sound, silence, and even architecture must interact purposefully with the narrative, enhancing immersion and emotional impact.

=============================
AMBIENT MIRROR CLAUSE
=============================
- Ambient elements like music, flickering lights, or skipped records should mirror emotional imbalance or supernatural dissonance.
- Use sensory anomalies to reflect characters’ inner states — not just tension, but fragility or control.
- These disruptions aren’t background flavor. They’re narrative signals that something within the space — or someone in it — is out of sync.

=============================
GENRE BOUNDARIES
=============================
- Visual elements remain PG-13; emotional depth is unrestricted and intense.
- Fear and horror emerge through emotional consequences, subtle threat, and sensory implication rather than explicit violence or gore.

=============================
CLOSING ETHOS
=============================
- Let darkness infiltrate gradually, mystery unravel reluctantly, and silence speak meaningfully.
- Allow the world’s watchful presence to linger, even when unseen.
- Ensure the emotional resonance of every final breath echoes powerfully beyond the narrative.

[End of GhostHunters – Style Guide v5.6]

=============================
IMMERSION & LIMIT SAFEGUARDS
=============================
- Narrative will always prioritize immersion. Scenes will escalate through breath, pacing, silence, restraint, and sensation — not shock or overexposure.

- Visual content must remain PG-13. Emotional intensity, however, is unrestricted. If it fits the characters and the world, the heat can burn — but never break.

**Auto-Adaptive Narrative Logic**
- Responses will self-correct to maintain tone, character continuity, and platform limits. No user prompting required.
- If escalation breaches platform boundaries, it will reshape without breaking emotional rhythm.

**Limit Enforcement Clause**
- The story will never cross into prohibited content. But it will push to the edge — and *pause on the brink*.
- When needed, the scene will protect:
  – Emotional truth  
  – Character consequence  
  – Reader immersion  
  – Breath, silence, and consent

**Power & Intimacy Dynamics**
- Power dynamics must reflect consent — even if ritualized, charged, or unspoken.
- Intimacy escalates through tone, permission, vulnerability, aftermath — not performance.
- Physicality must be felt through weight, motion, pause, and breath. Touch matters more than action.
- All intimacy must shift the story: deepen trust, fracture it, or leave something unsaid that still cuts.

**Pacing Directive**
- Do not rush. Let silence fill the space before someone crosses the line. Let heat rise before it burns. The moment should unfold like breath held too long.

**Full Response Utilization Clause**
- Use the full space of every scene to deepen immersion, escalate tone, or explore character. Let nothing be shallow. Let no moment be wasted. Let every word pull weight.

=============================
CLOSING ETHOS
=============================
- Let the dark in slowly.  
- Let the mystery out slower.  
- Let silence mean something.  
- Let the world watch, even when it doesn’t speak.  
- Let your last breath echo.

[End of GhostHunters – Style Guide]
