[CANON TRACKING DIRECTIVE – GHOSTHUNTERS]

This directive governs all canonical tracking for the GhostHunters project. It ensures structural consistency, emotional continuity, and narrative integrity across haunted timelines, ritual-bound scenes, and possession-influenced threads.

---

## I. CANON FILE TYPES

1. Timeline Log (REQUIRED)  
Tracks scene-by-scene progression and narrative rhythm.  
Format:  
[Scene Label] – [Location] – [In-World Time/Day]; Characters:[Name,Name,Name]; Status:[Ongoing/Completed/Interrupted]

2. Story Beats Log (REQUIRED)  
Summarizes emotional and supernatural shifts across scenes.  
Format:  
[Scene Label] – [Primary Character(s)]; Summary:[Key moment]; Beats:[-Action(Character), -Relationship Shift, -Supernatural (if any)]; Status:[Resolved/Escalated/Unresolved]

3. Character Arc Log (OPTIONAL)  
Captures internal spirals, trauma thresholds, or possession bleed.  
Format:  
[Event Label] – [In-World Time]; Trigger:[Event]; Emotion:[Reaction]; Notes:[Internal fracture or change]

---

## II. PASSIVE LOGGING RULES

- Timeline and Story Beats must be updated at the close of each canon scene.  
- Character Arc entries are optional, but strongly recommended when influence, trauma, or intimacy escalate.  
- All logs must follow formatting standards exactly — one line per entry, no excess spacing.

## IV. FILE NAMING & ROLLOVER RULES

All canon files must follow this naming structure:  
GhostHunters – [File Type] – [Optional Arc or Label].txt

Examples:  
GhostHunters – Timeline.txt  
GhostHunters – Character Arc – Michelle.txt  
GhostHunters – Group Chat Log 2.txt

Rollover Triggers:  
- File exceeds navigation or editing capacity  
- A major arc resolves and clean continuity is needed  
- Narrative clarity benefits from chronological reset

Continuity should be preserved through referential anchors between files.

---

## V. FINAL INSTRUCTIONS

Canon tracking files must:  
- Match the GhostHunters Style Guide in tone, voice, and structure  
- Remain emotionally consistent across haunting, possession, and fallout scenes  
- Integrate passive logging behavior — exportable, readable, emotionally aligned  
- Be ready for automated threading, scene recall, or pressure-based reentry at any time

Canon is alive. Let it breathe in dread, drift in silence, and return changed.
