[BATTLECHASERS – VANYR CALENDAR SYSTEM]

CALENDAR OF VANYR – THE WHEEL OF BURNING STARS

Vanyr’s calendar is structured around the arc of the two celestial bodies known as **Mirae** (The Wandering Flame) and **Rhendros** (The Veiled Eye). The year is divided into **ten months**, grouped into **five seasons**. Each month contains **36 days**, for a total of **360 days** per year. A rare **Day of Severance** occurs every six years, standing alone outside the cycle.

---

## SEASONS OF VANYR

### 1. **Flamewake** – Season of Rebirth and Fire (Months 1–2)
- Time of melting snows, returning light, and sky-fire storms
- Strongest in Emberwake and Veyra

### 2. **Greenrise** – Season of Growth and Song (Months 3–4)
- Planting season, rising magic, and hunts of promise
- Strongest in Aelreth and Cindral

### 3. **Highveil** – Season of Glory and Sun (Months 5–6)
- Brightest days, warfare, tournaments, and political ambition
- Peak season in Cindral’s Fall

### 4. **Duskfall** – Season of Rot and Harvest (Months 7–8)
- Darkening days, fungal growth, memory rites, and long farewells
- Deepest in The Reach Below

### 5. **Whispershade** – Season of Silence and Dream (Months 9–10)
- Coldest days, quiet prophecies, drifting sleep magic
- Dominant in Olorin and Gravemount

---

## MONTHS OF THE YEAR

1. **Ignara** – “First Fire” (Flamewake)
2. **Ashenel** – “The Cinder Crown” (Flamewake)
3. **Velwynd** – “Leafwind” (Greenrise)
4. **Thessalune** – “Moon of Song” (Greenrise)
5. **Solvane** – “Sunhold” (Highveil)
6. **Karrinth** – “The Sovereign Sky” (Highveil)
7. **Emberhusk** – “The Hollow Harvest” (Duskfall)
8. **Myricorne** – “Sporesong” (Duskfall)
9. **Noctyra** – “The Whispering Cold” (Whispershade)
10. **Virelace** – “The Dreamglass Moon” (Whispershade)

---

## UNIVERSAL CELEBRATIONS

- **Ignis Rites** (1st of Ignara) – Fire-kindling ceremonies across Vanyr to begin the year
- **Verdance Day** (12th of Velwynd) – Treeblessing and offering of green things
- **The Skybound Games** (10th–24th of Karrinth) – Regional tournaments, duels, and aerial races
- **Veilward** (1st of Emberhusk) – Honoring the dead before the harvest; major for Quiet Flame
- **Night of Pale Bells** (15th of Noctyra) – Ghosts walk; songs must be sung backward
- **Rhendros Eclipse** (1st of Virelace) – Celestial day of dreams, visions, and reversals

---

## REGIONAL FESTIVALS

### **Aelreth**
- **Crownbloom** (Thessalune 22) – Folk flower festival celebrating river spirits and minor nobility
- **Forge of Oaths** (Solvane 18) – Duels and vow-renewal for guilds and houses

### **Olorin**
- **Inkveil** (Virelace 5–10) – Scribes write prophecy fragments. Public readings on the final day.
- **Silenthymn** (Noctyra 9) – Entire cities go wordless. Judgment made by gesture alone.

### **Emberwake**
- **Ashlight Pilgrimage** (Ignara 20–Ashenel 4) – Pilgrims walk barefoot from Pyraeth to Ashhollow
- **Skykindle** (Solvane 3) – Bonding ritual for skyknights and their flamebeasts

### **Veyra Isles**
- **Tidecall** (Velwynd 9, Karrinth 9) – Double festivals honoring tidebound prophecies
- **Driftwake’s Run** (Karrinth 21) – Ship-race and masquerade on the sea

### **Cindral’s Fall**
- **Throne Dusting** (Every Emberhusk 30) – Ceremony reenacting the empire’s rise and fall
- **Iron Vow** (Karrinth 5) – Formal binding of new oaths; blood ink preferred

### **The Reach Below**
- **Bloomwake** (Myricorne 1–6) – Mushrooms sing. Citizens listen.
- **Echofast** (Virelace 22–end) – A fasting of sound. No song, speech, or step above a whisper.

---

## THE DAY OF SEVERANCE

Every six years, a single unnumbered day called the **Day of Severance** marks the old gods' turning. No law holds. No contract binds. Marriages are dissolved by kiss or cut. Memory bleeds backward. Most sleep through it — those who don’t are changed.
