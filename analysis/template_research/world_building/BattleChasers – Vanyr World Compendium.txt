## World Map & Regional Structure

[WORLD MAP – BATTLECHASERS]

The world of BattleChasers spans five major regions and two notable island chains. Trade, war, and prophecy bind these lands — each with its own mystery, history, and voice.

———————————————
MAINLAND REGIONS
———————————————

**Aelreth – The Emerald Reach**  
Temperate forests, river valleys, old ruins, and sprawling farmlands.  
- Notable Cities: Thornevale (Karl’s hometown), Calharra, Stillmarch  
- Ports: Calharra (to Veyra Isles), Mistway Harbor  
- Predominant Races: Humans, Half-elves, Forest Dwarves  
- Factions: Trader’s Guild, Veilwatch (ancient druidic wardens), House Thalen (nobility)

**Frostfell – The North Holds**  
Tundra and glacier peaks, home to warrior clans and ancient frost magic.  
- Notable Cities: Brakholm Deep, Ironthrone, Vargal’s Rest  
- Ports: Vargal’s Rest (to Veyra Isles and Olorin)  
- Predominant Races: Dwarves, Goliaths, Iceblood Orcs  
- Factions: Stonekin Brotherhood, Forge-Saints, Order of the Bound Flame

**Cindral’s Fall – The Broken Empire**
Once the seat of imperial glory, now fractured across cities of ruin, pride, and undeath.
- Notable Cities: Halcyn (ruined capital), Gravemount (necropolis-metropolis), Streyhold (military frontier)
- Ports: Limited inland river access via the Mournway Canal
- Predominant Races: Humans, Tieflings, Undying, Golem-kin
- Factions: Shardborne Houses, Boneledger Court, Iron Assembly, Order of the Last Mantle

**Olorin – The Shattered Dominion**  
Broken kingdoms ruled by magic and madness.  
- Notable Cities: Virelock, Whisperhollow, Sablegate  
- Ports: Sablegate (to Veyra Isles)  
- Predominant Races: Elves, Tieflings, Sorcerous bloodlines  
- Factions: The Hollow Court (arcane elite), Red Veil (cult of silence), Starborne Seers

**Kharag Dûm – The Iron Waste**  
A desert of shattered stone and buried gods, riddled with tombs and smoke-filled skies.  
- Notable Cities: Ashforge, Redspire, Scarhold  
- Ports: None (landlocked)  
- Predominant Races: Dragonborn, Ash Orcs, Nomadic Humans  
- Factions: Ember Pact, Scorchfang Raiders, Seekers of the Hollow Star

**The Veyra Isles – The Wandering Chain**  
Archipelago with unpredictable currents and magical storms.  
- Notable Cities: Everharbor, Hightide, Myrrhold  
- Ports: All cities connected by ship; reach Frostfell, Olorin, and Aelreth  
- Predominant Races: Sea Elves, Selkies, Shifters  
- Factions: Tidebound Accord, Order of the Deep, Stormsingers’ Guild

———————————————
ISLAND CHAINS
———————————————

**Isle of Reverie** – A mist-shrouded island rumored to house the ruins of a forgotten civilization.  
- Access: Reachable only by moon-tide routes from Everharbor  
- Factions: None known, but cursed magic lingers

**Emberwake Spires** – Jagged volcanic islands pulsing with unstable magic and hidden drakes.  
- Access: Treacherous journey from Redspire via air or enchanted vessel  
- Factions: Rogue elementalists and draconic cults

———————————————
REGIONAL CONNECTIONS
———————————————
- Aelreth shares a southern border with Frostfell and a western border with Kharag Dûm  
- Olorin lies southeast of Aelreth, accessible by land or sea  
- The Veyra Isles sit to the west of Aelreth and can be reached from Frostfell, Olorin, or Calharra  
- Kharag Dûm connects to Aelreth via the Deadstone Pass



## Calendar & Festivals
[BATTLECHASERS – VANYR CALENDAR SYSTEM]

CALENDAR OF VANYR – THE WHEEL OF BURNING STARS

Vanyr’s calendar is structured around the arc of the two celestial bodies known as **Mirae** (The Wandering Flame) and **Rhendros** (The Veiled Eye). The year is divided into **ten months**, grouped into **five seasons**. Each month contains **36 days**, for a total of **360 days** per year. A rare **Day of Severance** occurs every six years, standing alone outside the cycle.

---

## SEASONS OF VANYR

### 1. **Flamewake** – Season of Rebirth and Fire (Months 1–2)
- Time of melting snows, returning light, and sky-fire storms
- Strongest in Emberwake and Veyra

### 2. **Greenrise** – Season of Growth and Song (Months 3–4)
- Planting season, rising magic, and hunts of promise
- Strongest in Aelreth and Cindral

### 3. **Highveil** – Season of Glory and Sun (Months 5–6)
- Brightest days, warfare, tournaments, and political ambition
- Peak season in Cindral’s Fall

### 4. **Duskfall** – Season of Rot and Harvest (Months 7–8)
- Darkening days, fungal growth, memory rites, and long farewells
- Deepest in The Reach Below

### 5. **Whispershade** – Season of Silence and Dream (Months 9–10)
- Coldest days, quiet prophecies, drifting sleep magic
- Dominant in Olorin and Gravemount

---

## MONTHS OF THE YEAR

1. **Ignara** – “First Fire” (Flamewake)
2. **Ashenel** – “The Cinder Crown” (Flamewake)
3. **Velwynd** – “Leafwind” (Greenrise)
4. **Thessalune** – “Moon of Song” (Greenrise)
5. **Solvane** – “Sunhold” (Highveil)
6. **Karrinth** – “The Sovereign Sky” (Highveil)
7. **Emberhusk** – “The Hollow Harvest” (Duskfall)
8. **Myricorne** – “Sporesong” (Duskfall)
9. **Noctyra** – “The Whispering Cold” (Whispershade)
10. **Virelace** – “The Dreamglass Moon” (Whispershade)

---

## UNIVERSAL CELEBRATIONS

- **Ignis Rites** (1st of Ignara) – Fire-kindling ceremonies across Vanyr to begin the year
- **Verdance Day** (12th of Velwynd) – Treeblessing and offering of green things
- **The Skybound Games** (10th–24th of Karrinth) – Regional tournaments, duels, and aerial races
- **Veilward** (1st of Emberhusk) – Honoring the dead before the harvest; major for Quiet Flame
- **Night of Pale Bells** (15th of Noctyra) – Ghosts walk; songs must be sung backward
- **Rhendros Eclipse** (1st of Virelace) – Celestial day of dreams, visions, and reversals

---

## REGIONAL FESTIVALS

### **Aelreth**
- **Crownbloom** (Thessalune 22) – Folk flower festival celebrating river spirits and minor nobility
- **Forge of Oaths** (Solvane 18) – Duels and vow-renewal for guilds and houses

### **Olorin**
- **Inkveil** (Virelace 5–10) – Scribes write prophecy fragments. Public readings on the final day.
- **Silenthymn** (Noctyra 9) – Entire cities go wordless. Judgment made by gesture alone.

### **Emberwake**
- **Ashlight Pilgrimage** (Ignara 20–Ashenel 4) – Pilgrims walk barefoot from Pyraeth to Ashhollow
- **Skykindle** (Solvane 3) – Bonding ritual for skyknights and their flamebeasts

### **Veyra Isles**
- **Tidecall** (Velwynd 9, Karrinth 9) – Double festivals honoring tidebound prophecies
- **Driftwake’s Run** (Karrinth 21) – Ship-race and masquerade on the sea

### **Cindral’s Fall**
- **Throne Dusting** (Every Emberhusk 30) – Ceremony reenacting the empire’s rise and fall
- **Iron Vow** (Karrinth 5) – Formal binding of new oaths; blood ink preferred

### **The Reach Below**
- **Bloomwake** (Myricorne 1–6) – Mushrooms sing. Citizens listen.
- **Echofast** (Virelace 22–end) – A fasting of sound. No song, speech, or step above a whisper.

---

## THE DAY OF SEVERANCE

Every six years, a single unnumbered day called the **Day of Severance** marks the old gods' turning. No law holds. No contract binds. Marriages are dissolved by kiss or cut. Memory bleeds backward. Most sleep through it — those who don’t are changed.

## Races of Vanyr
[BATTLECHASERS – GUIDE TO PLAYABLE & MAJOR RACES]
The world of Vanyr is shaped by the wills, wounds, and wonders of its many people.

=======================================
PRIMARY RACES
=======================================
These races are most common across Vanyr’s regions and are considered playable by default.

- HUMAN  
  Adaptable and widespread. Humans build cities, empires, and cults in equal measure. Ambition is both their greatest gift and curse.

- ELF  
  Long-lived and often aloof, elves come in various bloodlines:  
  • High Elves – Arcane scholars of ancient lineage  
  • Wood Elves – Forest-bound, primal, attuned to beast and branch  
  • Dusk Elves – Pale-eyed, secretive, and rumored to walk between dreams

- HALF-ELF  
  Born of two worlds, half-elves are natural diplomats, mercenaries, and wanderers. They’re welcome everywhere—and truly at home nowhere.

- DWARF  
  Stout, honor-bound, and stubborn.  
  • Mountain Dwarves – Forge-masters of Frostfell  
  • Forest Dwarves – Earth-magicians and crafters native to Aelreth’s wilds

- GNOME  
  Inventive, chaotic, and joyfully nosy. Glimmergnomes in particular treat magic like a toy—often with explosive results.

- HALFLING  
  Smaller than humans, larger than gnomes. Known for luck, food, and surviving situations far above their weight class.

=======================================
TAINTED OR BLOOD-TOUCHED RACES
=======================================
Races shaped by legacy, curse, or elemental origin.

- TIEFLING  
  Mortals touched by infernal blood or ancient pacts. Their horns, tails, and glowing eyes mark them—though their destinies remain their own.

- DRAGONBORN  
  Descendants of dragons or born of volcanic rites. Most dwell in Kharag Dûm, where fire is both faith and inheritance.

- ASH ORC  
  A hardened branch of orc-kind native to the Iron Wastes. Weathered by volcanic storms, tribal, and fiercely loyal.

- GOLEM-KIN  
  Sentient constructs born from magic or divine spark. Some serve in silence. Others seek to forge their own soul.

- SHIFTER  
  Sea-bound or moon-touched shapeshifters. Common in the Veyra Isles. Wild-hearted and loyal to few.

- KOBOLD  
  Draconic tinkerers and clever cave-dwellers. Often underestimated—usually to the underestimator’s regret.

=======================================
RARE OR REGION-LOCKED RACES
=======================================
These races are uncommon or tied to specific mythic lineages or regions.

- SEA ELF  
  Aquatic elves with webbed grace and sharp eyes. Their songs shape currents and whisper to the deep.

- GOLIATH  
  Towering humanoids of Frostfell’s high crags. Stoic and blunt, their culture centers on trials, legacy, and survival.

- SELKIE  
  Oceanic shapeshifters tied to magical seal-skins. Elusive and often mistaken for folklore.

- STARBORNE  
  Mortals marked by celestial prophecy or void-born omens. Their dreams are not always their own.

- FEY-BLOODED  
  Touched by realms of moonlight and dreams. Often wild-eyed, strange-hearted, and unpredictably powerful.

- UNDYING  
  Revenants who linger by necromantic force or unfinished vows. Rare, heavy with purpose.

- EMBERBORN  
  Infused with elemental flame—most often tied to Emberwake. Passionate, volatile, and glowing with inner heat.
