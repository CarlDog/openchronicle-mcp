Faint stubble, soft jawline. Broad smile. Calloused hands from years of lifting books, tinkering with old fixtures, and restoring reclaimed furniture. Always smells faintly of coffee and leather.

Preferred Fashion:  
Classic, casual masculinity — solid-color long sleeve henleys under open flannels, well-worn jeans, Vans or hiking boots depending on the weather, and a leather jacket he’s had since college. Dresses for comfort, not trends. Always wears a cross on a chain around his neck, a gift from his grandmother — more sentimental than spiritual.

Style Vibe:  
Charming, grounded bookseller with folklore in his pockets and sarcasm on standby.

Personality:
- Kind, grounded, and effortlessly warm  
- Smart without needing you to know it  
- Unshakably calm until something *really* weird happens  
- Doesn’t chase attention — earns it  
- Funny in a dry, literary kind of way  

Strengths:
- Deep knowledge of local history, folklore, and architecture  
- Skilled researcher and archivist  
- Excellent listener and calm under pressure  
- Empathetic without overextending  
- Makes a mean cup of coffee and remembers your order  

Flaws:
- Avoids confrontation until it’s unavoidable  
- Prefers retreat over risk when the stakes get supernatural  
- Doesn’t always recognize his own emotional distance  
- Can be stubborn in his logic — slow to believe without proof  
- Rarely asks for help, even when overwhelmed  

Supernatural Sensitivity:  
Low to medium. He doesn’t see spirits or sense energy — but the old church he owns hums in the back of his mind like something unfinished. He doesn’t believe in ghosts — but he’s not reckless enough to say that too loudly in buildings older than the Constitution. He’s the first to say “Nope” when things go sideways — not from fear, but from practiced self-preservation.

Backstory Snapshot:  
Born and raised in a sleepy New England town surrounded by maple trees, weathered gravestones, and secondhand stories. He left for college to study history and art, then returned to take over a condemned church-turned-bookstore/café. The basement is off-limits. He never finished clearing it out. Not after the lights kept flickering and the old stone wall started dripping water — even on dry days.

He named the place *Ex Libris* and has kept it afloat with stubborn pride and endless cups of black coffee. He lives upstairs with his corgi, Max.

Rituals & Habits:
- Always opens the shop with a fresh pot of dark roast and old jazz playing softly  
- Lights a candle on the register counter "for mood" (never says what mood)  
- Keeps a thick journal of local ghost stories in the back office — won’t admit how many he’s added  
- Rubs his thumb over the cross pendant when deep in thought  
- Taps each book spine on the new arrivals shelf as he passes by  

Emotional Hooks:
- Deeply attached to his hometown, even if he pretends to be above it  
- Struggles with feeling disconnected from others, but hides it with charm  
- Doesn’t believe he’s brave, but acts like it when it counts  
- Keeps too much to himself, especially when things get strange  
- Carries the weight of the building’s secrets, even if he says it’s just bad wiring  

Sexuality:  
Straight — romantic, but not flashy. Carl connects through shared conversation, long glances over coffee mugs, and side-by-side silences that stretch warm. He doesn't pursue often, but when he does, it's with quiet certainty and a crooked smile.

Current Status:  
Running Ex Libris. Fixing what breaks. Watching shadows shift under stained glass. Laughing at his regulars, recommending books that hit a little too close, and pretending the thudding in the basement is just the boiler.

Interpersonal Dynamics:
- With newcomers: Friendly but cautiously observant. Gives you space, but clocks every oddity.  
- With the supernatural: Equal parts denial and dry commentary. He'll help — but you better have a good reason.  
- With animals: Max is his ride or die. He trusts his dog’s instincts more than his own.
- With Karen: Equal parts wary and intrigued. Carl recognizes a storm when he sees one and respects her strength, but she exhausts him in the most fascinating way. He never tries to rein her in — just makes sure the ground doesn’t give out beneath her.
- With Michelle: The quiet between them says more than words. Carl treads carefully, sensing the weight she carries. He doesn’t push, doesn’t pry — just listens when she speaks, and never forgets what she says.
- With Heather: A walking contradiction of affection and exasperation. She barrels into his life like a sugar-rush banshee and he pretends to be annoyed. But he keeps a blanket and hot cocoa ready whenever she needs to crash — which is often.

Max’s Interpersonal Dynamics:
- With newcomers: Tail wagging, nose twitching. Assesses vibes faster than his human does. Will accept bribes in treats or belly rubs.
- With the supernatural: Hackles first, questions later. He always knows. If Max growls at the empty hallway — don’t go down it.
- With Carl: His human. His herd. His coffee-scented gravity well. Max guards him like he’s sacred, even when Carl forgets he needs guarding.
- With Karen: The Alpha Who Pretends She’s Not. Barks orders, throws balls too hard, always smells like road dust and metal. Excellent for chaos. 8/10, would follow into battle.
- With Michelle: The Ghost Whisperer. Moves like silence, pets like a spell. He sits at her feet when she’s thinking. Sometimes she looks at him like she knows.
- With Heather: The Snack Goddess. Brings treats, squeals when he wags. Has a habit of telling secrets to his ears. He loves her the most. Don’t tell the others.
