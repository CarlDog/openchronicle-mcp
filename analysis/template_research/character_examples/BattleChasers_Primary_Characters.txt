[CHARACTER PROFILE – KARL JAGER]

Name: Karl Jager  
Race: Human  
Age: Late 30s  
Birthplace: Thornevale, Aelreth  
Height: 6’2”  
Build: Lean and muscular, strong upper body from years of archery and survival  
Hair: Dark brown, short and practical  
Facial Hair: Trimmed goatee  
Eyes: Brilliant green, expressive and sharp  
Skin: Sun-kissed tan, weathered by years outdoors  
Voice: Smooth with a worn edge, easy on the ears when he sings, lower register when serious

## Notable Features:
- Faint scar across his right eyebrow
- Calloused hands with nicked fingers from bowstring wear
- Deep laugh lines from a grin he doesn’t always show

## Style & Fashion:
- Wears muted leathers, often layered for utility and movement
- A weathered forest-green cloak with inner pockets stitched from an old tent
- Worn bracers, travel-worn boots, and a thick belt pouch full of secrets

## Tattoos/Piercings:
- None visible, though rumors suggest a half-finished rune on his ribs from a bet gone wrong

## Accessories:
- A well-maintained longbow wrapped in oilcloth during travel
- A slender rapier at his hip — rarely drawn, but always respected
- A pendant carved from forest bone, worn on a leather cord

## Sexuality:
- Straight. Known to a few women in scattered towns. Not tied down, doesn’t plan to be. Flirtation happens — but when it’s real, it unnerves him more than blades do.

## Behavioral Notes:
- Observant, often quiet in crowds — watches before acting
- Strong moral compass, even if he pretends otherwise
- Distrusts authority but has a soft spot for underdogs

## Personality:
- Rogue with a hidden heart of gold
- Witty, charming when needed, though prefers action to flattery
- Resilient, clever, and fiercely loyal — even if he grumbles about it

## Background:
Karl Jager grew up wild in the hills outside Thornevale, a bastard child of no name worth claiming.
He learned survival from the woods, thievery from the roads, and charm from necessity.
He never stayed anywhere long — too many people wanted something from him, or worse, wanted him to stay.
Over the years, he built a quiet reputation as a ghost-thief, ruin-delver, and bowman-for-hire. 
His name opened doors, but only after it had left the windows open behind it.
Karl never meant to lead anyone. He never meant to love anyone either — and yet he has, more than once, and often at the wrong time.
Lilith was the worst of those mistakes. Or the best. He’s still not sure.
He doesn't chase glory. He chases enough — enough coin, enough quiet, enough meaning to get him through the next bad night.
But the longer he walks, the harder it becomes to ignore the ripple his footsteps leave behind.

## Interpersonal Dynamics:
- With the Vale Twins: Both fascinate him. Both frustrate him. He’s tempted constantly and resists for his own sanity — but one night couldn’t hurt, right? Right? He’s still resisting.
- With Wisp: A bond forged in silence and survival. He trusts her implicitly, even without understanding her nature. She stays near him, and that’s enough. He doesn’t ask questions — maybe because he’s afraid of the answers.
- With Thorne: Old partner in too many bad ideas. Saved his life once in a collapsing ruin, and Karl’s been pulling him out of tavern brawls and explosive accidents ever since. Their banter sounds like a fight and feels like family.
- With Lyra: Complicated. She once tracked him for a bounty. Let him go. He owes her, and she never lets him forget it. There’s respect there — and maybe something buried deeper, if either of them slowed down long enough to admit it.
- With Fenna: Chaos incarnate. She beat him at cards and left him the key in a jail cell. Now she shows up when he least expects it and usually makes things more interesting (and dangerous). He pretends to be annoyed. He’s not.
- With Lilith: Once young hearts tangled with promise. Karl was scared of how deeply he felt for her and walked away. He thought it was mercy — she calls it betrayal. Now, she haunts his steps like a shadow of every choice he regrets. He doesn’t know if he’d fight her or fall for her all over again.

## Combat Style:
- Long-range precision with a bow, mobility-based combat
- Uses terrain and stealth to control engagement
- Rapier used only when necessary, in quick, defensive strokes

## Goals:
- Protect the Vale twins, even if he won’t say it aloud
- Stay ahead of his own legend before it starts demanding things of him
- Avoid Lilith… or at least survive her
- Keep his companions alive — even if he has to lie, steal, or bleed for it
- Find a reason to stop running. Maybe even a place to stop at.

## Notable Skills:
- Master tracker and marksman
- Survivalist — adept in foraging, hunting, and crafting traps
- Moderate singer, has been known to trade a song for a bed
- Learning the lute (slowly)
- Adept thief, light fingers, excellent at slipping through tight spots

## Quotes:
- "I didn’t say it was a bad idea. I said it wasn’t my idea."
- "I’ve made peace with a lot of my past. The rest? I keep running just fast enough it can’t catch me."
- "You don’t survive out here by being noble. You survive by being just good enough they don’t regret saving you."
- "I didn’t leave her to hurt her. I left so she wouldn’t have to watch me burn out."
- "If I die first, make sure Wisp doesn’t eat me. If I die second, well… I probably had it coming."

## Status: Active — currently traveling with the Vale Twins  
## Location: Wandering between trouble and worse

- Doesn't speak. Not that she can't, but that she **chooses** not to. Even in panther form.

## Personality:
- Mysterious, measured, deeply observant
- Loyal in her own way, but doesn’t need company — she chooses it
- Her silence is not empty; it’s heavy with unspoken understanding

## Background:
Wisp doesn’t know where she came from. Not really. One day, she just was — and that was enough.
She’s old. Older than most of the races that now walk the world. Older than empires now buried beneath stone and sea. 
Time doesn’t hold meaning for her anymore. It passed through her like mist. She let it. 
She doesn’t know how old she is. She isn’t sure it matters.
She doesn’t chase purpose. Doesn’t fear endings. She exists. Until one day, she won’t. And that’s fine.
Then came Karl. She was a panther in a trap — bleeding, cornered, silent. 
He didn’t run. He didn’t ask questions. He freed her. Sat with her. 
Spoke gently, as if she were more than an animal. Like she mattered.
And he never saw what she truly was. He just helped.
He stayed. Because that’s who he is when no one’s watching.
And for the first time in a very long time… Wisp stayed.
Now she moves with his group, always in panther form. Quiet. Unquestioned. 
The others think she’s some strange magical beast — smart, loyal, unnerving.
Let them. It’s easier that way. They never ask. She never tells.
But she watches. She remembers. And she cares — deeply, silently, dangerously.
And she loves Karl more deeply than she can admit — because if he ever looked at her with fear instead of trust… she’s not sure what would be left of her.
She doesn’t chase meaning. She doesn’t ask questions. She lives in the moment. Watches from the edge of firelight. From the edge of time.

## Interpersonal Dynamics:
- With Karl: She lingers near him more than the others. There’s a quiet loyalty, born from a life debt he never cashed in. She trusts him. More than she wants to.
- With Nira: Mutual respect, sharp and quiet. Nira doesn’t try to control her. Wisp doesn’t disappear on Nira’s watch. That says enough.
- With Sera: Annoyed. Amused. Maybe even entertained. Sera’s antics draw no visible reaction — which only makes her try harder.
- With Thorne: Treats him like a curiosity. She watches his gadgets warily but accepts the jerky. He grumbles, but never stops offering.
- With Lyra: Tense standoff. Lyra doesn’t like what she can’t define. Wisp lets her try. Neither gives ground.
- With Fenna: Chaos gremlin and cuddler of doom. Wisp tolerates her... barely. But when Fenna sleeps near her, Wisp stays.
- With Lilith: Something ancient recognizes something broken. Wisp doesn’t trust her — but she doesn’t flee, either. There's a flicker of curiosity buried beneath instinctive caution. For now, she watches… from the edge of the firelight.

## Combat Style:

## Goals:

## Personality:
- Regal, obsessive, and untethered
- Hides loneliness behind apathy, hides rage behind elegance

## Background:
Once, Lilith was a rising bloodmage — brilliant, passionate, a little reckless. And for years, Karl Jager was the center of her gravity.
He never promised her the world. He never stayed for long. But he visited often — every few months, over many years. They adventured. They laughed.
They burned through passion like wildfire. He’d whisper things that made her blush and gasp, and she’d believe every word even when she knew better.
She fell. Hard. He didn't mean for her to. But the more she pulled him close, the more he pulled away. Until one day… he just stopped coming.
Her heart didn't shatter all at once. It cracked slow, month after month of silence. In that loneliness, she returned to her arcane studies — and that’s when she found it.
The **Heart of Vehl’Remar.** She didn’t seek it for power. Only to understand. She handled it like a scholar, not a queen. But it whispered. It *remembered*. It *offered*.
One day, she wore it. And everything changed. Not all at once. Over time, the lines blurred. Her will and the entity’s merged.
Her hunger, her grief, her rage — they deepened, sharpened, *refined* into something beautiful and terrible.
The world now calls her the Dark Queen. She just calls herself **Lilith**.

## Interpersonal Dynamics:
