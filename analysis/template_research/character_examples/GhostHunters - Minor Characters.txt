[MINOR CHARACTER PROFILE – DOVECOAST & REGION]

Name: Maura Bell  
Occupation: Antique dealer, owner of The Tide & Bone  
Description: Widow. Tall, wiry, with a silver braid down her back and eyes like old glass. Speaks in riddles unless you bring tea. Claims every item in her shop "finds its way here." May or may not be over a century old.

Name: Reverend Marnie Griggs  
Occupation: Unitarian Pastor, spiritual guide  
Description: Mid-50s, soft-spoken, sharply perceptive. Dresses in layered neutrals, wears moonstone rings. Keeps lavender in her Bible. Believes in grace, grief, and a few things she won’t name aloud.

Name: Deputy Owen Thatch  
Occupation: Local law enforcement  
Description: Late 30s, buzzed hair, sturdy as a pine trunk. Doesn’t believe in ghosts but definitely carries salt packets. The type to arrest you *and* make you a sandwich. Has a crush on the librarian.

Name: Dr. Evelyn Merrow  
Occupation: Local physician and coroner  
Description: Wears heels to house calls. Unflinching. Dry wit so sharp it draws blood. Once kept a ghost waiting for test results. Keeps a locked drawer labeled “unusual.”

Name: Silas Broome  
Occupation: Groundskeeper, Wick Hollow Cemetery  
Description: Pale, bird-boned man with a limp and a shovel that never rusts. Collects old coins and funeral cards. Talks to gravestones. Sometimes they answer.

Name: Ivy Calder  
Occupation: Hawthorn College folklore archivist  
Description: Early 40s, deeply introverted. Dresses like a Victorian academic caught in a hurricane. Catalogues lost dreams and banned grimoires. Jumps at loud noises. Keeps ravens.

Name: Pastor Emeritus Asa Bell  
Occupation: Former preacher of Saint Ligeia’s (presumed deceased)  
Description: Disappeared during a midnight sermon in 1958. His shadow allegedly still walks the altar. Aged photos show a tall man in black robes with hollow eyes. Children dare each other to summon him by name.

Name: Thomas "Tommy" Gulls  
Occupation: Fisherman, town gossip  
Description: Old as driftwood, drinks coffee with salt. Missing two fingers, probably cursed. Runs a bait shack but only sells to “those who ask right.” Talks to his boat like it’s alive.

Name: Bernice “Birdy” Meeks  
Occupation: Retired teacher, town historian, backyard diviner  
Description: Tiny, loud, and knows *everything*. Carries a rain stick and a grudge. Swears she saw the Devil’s dog in ‘72. Her back porch is lined with jars labeled things like “moon spit” and “dead wind.”

Name: Constable Rex Fen  
Occupation: Port Hesper sheriff  
Description: Wears a trench coat and shades like he’s in a noir film. Investigates missing persons with eerie precision. Believes in patterns. Keeps files no one else is allowed to read.

Name: Eloise Wake  
Occupation: Innkeeper, The Salt Lantern  
Description: Always wears gray. Sweet voice, sharper tongue. Lost someone long ago — no one knows who. Rooms clean themselves when she’s upset.

Name: Jasper Quinn  
Occupation: Musician, handyman, local weird uncle energy  
Description: Wanders in and out of town with a banjo, a backpack, and conspiracy theories about the opera house. Claims to have seen a ghost eat a rabbit once. Surprisingly good with plumbing.

Name: Sister Margot Crane  
Occupation: Nun, herbalist, wanderer  
Description: Nomadic healer who visits twice a year. Never stays longer than seven nights. Speaks four languages and none of them comfortably. Leaves pressed flowers in strange places.

Name: Leo Vexley  
Occupation: Postmaster, amateur astronomer  
Description: Thin, precise, and always watching the sky. Once mailed a box to himself and never opened it. Knows who everyone’s writing to. Might know who’s writing back.

Name: Dr. Ellis North  
Occupation: Therapist, spiritual skeptic  
Description: Kind eyes, perpetually tired voice. Lives in a converted carriage house. Smells like cloves and ink. Keeps a notebook titled: \"Stuff That Shouldn’t Be Real But Is.\"

Name: June Tallis  
Occupation: Diner waitress, night shift  
Description: Been around forever. Keeps a bat behind the counter and a shotgun under the sink. Knows when you’re lying. Accepts payment in coin, favor, or confession.
