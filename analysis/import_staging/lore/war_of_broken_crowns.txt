The War of Broken Crowns (Third Age, Years 847-854)

This devastating conflict lasted seven years and marked the end of the Third Age. Three powerful kingdoms - Valeria, Drakmoor, and the Northern Reaches - fought for control of the Eternal Flame, a legendary artifact said to grant immortality to its wielder.

The war began when the Flame was discovered in the ruins of the ancient city of Aethros. Each kingdom claimed rightful ownership based on different historical precedents:
- Valeria: Descendants of the Flame's original creators
- Drakmoor: Conquered the city where it was found
- Northern Reaches: Possessed the key to unlock its power

Key battles included:
- The Siege of Ironhold (Year 849): Valeria's greatest fortress falls
- The Battle of Crimson Fields (Year 851): Massive casualties on all sides
- The Sundering of Aethros (Year 854): The final, catastrophic battle

The war ended when Prince Aldwin of Valeria, in desperation, shattered the Eternal Flame rather than let it fall into enemy hands. The destruction released a magical wave that forever changed the world, beginning the current Age of Shadows. The Flame's pieces were scattered across the realm, becoming objects of legend and quests for future generations.
